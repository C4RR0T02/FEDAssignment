History of The Cecil Hotel


Opened in 1927 in downtown Los Angeles, the Cecil Hotel is one of the most infamous buildings plagued with at least 16 different murders, suicides and unexplained paranormal events. Due to the great depression between 1929 and 1939, the surrounding of the Cecil Hotel was dubbed as the 'skid row', being home to thousands of homeless individuals.

The reputation of the Cecil Hotel than took a turn for the worst earning the reputation for violence and death. Reports of residents ingesting poison, shooting themselves and even slitting their throat and even jumping out of the apartments bedroom windows. In September 1944, a 19-year-old lady awoke in the middle of the night with stomach pains while she was staying with a 38 year old man. She went to the bathroom unknowingly giving birth to a baby boy. Thinking that the baby boy was dead, she threw the baby out of the window onto the roof of the neighbouring building. Apart from the suicides, the Cecil was also a serial killer paradise. Serial Killers like Richard Ramirez also known as the 'Night Stalker' stayed on the top floor of the complex during his killing spree.

In the year 2011, due to Cecil's Hotel reputation, the management decided to rename and split up the Cecil Hotel into 2 hotels. Stay-on-Main' and the Cecil Hotel. The Stay-on-Main mainly catered towards tourists and the Cecil catered towards the more permanent residents. Not long after the rebranding, the Stay-on-Main struck its first tragedy. On January 26 2013, a 21-year-old Canadian student Elisa Lam checked into the Stay-on-Main. She was originally staying in a hostel-style accommodations along with her other roommates before getting complaints. Elisa decided it was a better idea staying alone hence, she moved into a room by herself. On the 31st of January, Elisa Lam was declared missing. There were eerie videos of Elisa Lam entering and looking around the elevator on the day of her disappearance. Three Weeks later, her body was found in one of the water tanks of the building after guests reported the water coming from the tap tasting different and a lower water pressure.
